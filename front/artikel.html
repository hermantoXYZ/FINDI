Roblox is rolling out face ID age checks to stop older players from chatting to kids, but will it actually work?

PCGamesN Published 14 hours ago

Roblox is introducing a new age verification system that uses face ID and government-issued identification to ensure users are the age they claim to be. The move is intended to prevent older players from interacting with younger users in inappropriate ways, but privacy advocates and some users are concerned about the collection of biometric data.

The new system, which is optional, will be available to users who are 13 years of age or older. Those who choose to verify their age will have a badge on their profile indicating that they have done so. Roblox says that the system will help to create a safer and more "age-appropriate" experience for all users.

However, some are skeptical that the system will be effective. Critics point out that users can still lie about their age when creating an account, and that there are ways to bypass the face ID system. There are also concerns about how Roblox will store and protect the sensitive data it collects.

The company has stated that it will use a "third-party identity verification service" to handle the data, and that it will not store any of the biometric information itself. However, this has not been enough to assuage the concerns of all critics.

It remains to be seen whether Roblox's new age verification system will be successful in making the platform a safer place for kids. However, it is a clear sign that the company is taking the issue of child safety seriously.
